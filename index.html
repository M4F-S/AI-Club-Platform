About the club

Our mission, principles, and why we exist.

Our mission

We create a space where students can develop practical AI skills through workshops, peer learning, project demos, study sessions, and collaborative building, while also understanding how to use AI thoughtfully, ethically, and effectively in everyday coding and problem-solving.

The club is open to students of all levels and supports learning, experimentation, and community growth at 42 Berlin, in alignment with the school’s peer-to-peer model and community standards.

Club principles

  • We are student-led and community-oriented.
  • We focus on learning, experimentation, and responsible AI use.
  • We stay vendor-neutral and welcome support from multiple AI organizations.
  • We respect 42 Berlin’s community standards and privacy expectations.
  • We do not use the club for spam, commercial promotion, or unrelated marketing.

What we do

Hands-on learning for every level.

Workshops

Host AI workshops for beginners and advanced members.

Prompt engineering

Run prompt engineering sessions and practical AI tool demos.

Projects

Build small AI projects together in teams or individually.

Responsible AI

Discuss AI safety, responsible use, privacy, and bias.

Guest speakers

Invite guest speakers, mentors, and industry partners when available.

Resources

Share resources and best practices for using AI in study, coding, and project work.

Why this club exists

AI is becoming a core skill for coding, product building, and problem solving, and many students want a structured place to learn how to use it well. This club helps 42 Berlin students build real practical skills while also understanding the limits, risks, and responsibilities of AI use.

By combining hands-on learning with responsible guidance, we want to make AI accessible, useful, and safe for the whole community.
